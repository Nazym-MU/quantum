Show, using matrix representations in the Sz basis, that $[J_x, J_y] = iJ_z$. Recall that $J_x$ in that basis was found in Townsendâ€™s problem 2.10.

## Solution
## Commutator:
$$\hat{J}_x = \frac{\hbar}{2}\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \qquad \hat{J}_y = \frac{\hbar}{2}\begin{bmatrix} 0 & -i \\ i & 0 \end{bmatrix}$$
$$\hat{J}_x\hat{J}_y = \frac{\hbar^2}{4}\begin{bmatrix} i & 0 \\ 0 & -i \end{bmatrix}$$
$$\hat{J}_y\hat{J}_x = \frac{\hbar^2}{4}\begin{bmatrix} -i & 0 \\ 0 & i \end{bmatrix}$$
$$\hat{J}_x\hat{J}_y - \hat{J}_y\hat{J}_x = \frac{\hbar^2}{4}\begin{bmatrix} 2i & 0 \\ 0 & -2i \end{bmatrix} = \frac{i\hbar^2}{2}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} = i\hbar\hat{J}_z$$
because we know that $\hat{J}_z = \frac{\hbar}{2}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix}$
