In Problem 3.2 we will see that the state of a spin-Â½ particle that is spin up along the axis whose direction is specified by the unit vector
$$\mathbf{n} = \sin\theta \cos\phi\,\mathbf{i} + \sin\theta \sin\phi\,\mathbf{j} + \cos\theta\,\mathbf{k}$$

with $\theta$ and $\phi$ shown in Fig. 1.11, is given by
$$|+n\rangle = \cos\frac{\theta}{2}\,|+z\rangle + e^{i\phi}\sin\frac{\theta}{2}\,|-z\rangle$$
(a) Verify that the state $|+n\rangle$ reduces to the states $|+x\rangle$ and $|+y\rangle$ given in this chapter for the appropriate choice of the angles $\theta$ and $\phi$.
(b) Suppose that a measurement of $S_z$ is carried out on a particle in the state $|+n\rangle$.  
What is the probability that the measurement yields  
- (i) $+\hbar/2$?  
- (ii) $-\hbar/2$?
(c) Determine the uncertainty $\Delta S_z$ of your measurements.
---
## Solution
---
a) $\ket{+x} \quad \to \quad \theta=\pi/2, \phi=0$
$$\ket{+x} = \cos\frac{\pi}{4}\ket{+z} + e^0\sin\frac{\pi}{4}\ket{-z} = \frac{1}{\sqrt2}\ket{+z}+\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\ket{-z}$$
$\ket{+y} \quad \to \quad \theta=\pi/2, \phi=\pi/2$
$$\ket{+x} = \cos\frac{\pi}{4}\ket{+z} + e^{i\pi/2}\sin\frac{\pi}{4}\ket{-z} = \frac{1}{\sqrt2}\ket{+z}+\frac{i}{\sqrt2}\ket{-z}$$
because $e^{i\pi/2} = \cos\frac{\pi}{2} + i\sin\frac{\pi}{2}=i$

---
b) Probabilities are given by the product of the complex conjugate of the amplitude and the amplitude. The momenta $+\hbar/2$ and $-\hbar/2$ of measured $S_z$ correspond to the eigenvalues of states $\ket{+z}$ and $\ket{-z}$. Therefore, we need to find the inner products:$
$$\braket{+z|+n} = \cos\frac{\theta}{2}\,\braket{+z|+z} + e^{i\phi}\sin\frac{\theta}{2}\,\braket{+z|-z}=\cos\frac{\theta}{2}$$
$$\braket{-z|+n} = \cos\frac{\theta}{2}\,\braket{-z|+z} + e^{i\phi}\sin\frac{\theta}{2}\,\braket{-z|-z}=e^{i\phi}\sin\frac{\theta}{2}$$
The probabilities are:
$$|\braket{+z|+n}|^2 = \braket{+n|+z}\braket{+z|+n}=\cos^2\frac{\theta}{2}$$
$$|\braket{-z|+n}|^2 = \braket{+n|-z}\braket{-z|+n}=\sin^2\frac{\theta}{2}$$
---
c) To find the uncertainty, first find the expectation values (probability-weighted averages):
$$\braket{S_z}  =\cos^2\frac{\theta}{2}\left(+\frac{\hbar}{2}\right)+\sin^2\frac{\theta}{2}\left(-\frac{\hbar}{2}\right)=\left(\cos^2\theta - \sin^2\theta\right)\frac{\hbar}{2}=\cos\theta\frac{\hbar}{2}$$
$$\braket{S_z^2}  =\cos^2\frac{\theta}{2}\left(+\frac{\hbar^2}{4}\right)+\sin^2\frac{\theta}{2}\left(\frac{\hbar^2}{4}\right)=\left(\cos^2\theta + \sin^2\theta\right)\frac{\hbar^2}{4}=\frac{\hbar^2}{4}$$
$$\Delta S_z = \sqrt{\braket{S_z^2}-\braket{S_z}^2}=\sqrt{\frac{h^2}{4} -\cos^2\theta\frac{\hbar^2}{4}} = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar^2}{4}\left( 1-\cos^2\theta\right)} =\frac{\hbar}{2}\sin\theta$$
