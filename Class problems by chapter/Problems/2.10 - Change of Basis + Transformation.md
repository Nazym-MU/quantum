Determine the matrix representation of $\hat{J}_x$ in the $S_z$ basis.  
**Suggestion:** Start with the matrix representation of the operator $\hat{S}_x$ using the states
$$
|+x\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}|+z\rangle + \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}|-z\rangle
$$
$$
|-x\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}|+z\rangle - \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}|-z\rangle
$$
as a basis, and then transform to the $S_z$ basis.

---
Transformation matrix from $S_z$ to $S_x$ basis is:
$$\begin{bmatrix} 1/\sqrt{2} && 1/\sqrt{2} \\ 1/\sqrt{2} && -1/\sqrt{2} \end{bmatrix}$$
Matrix representation of $J_x$ in the $S_x$ basis:
$$\begin{bmatrix} \hbar/2 && 0 \\ 0 && -\hbar/2 \end{bmatrix}$$
Find the transformation basis using $[S_x]_z = U^\dagger [S_x]_z U$

$$=\frac{\hbar}{4}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}=\frac{\hbar}{2}\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}$$
