Find the matrix representation of $\hat{R}(\varphi)$ in the $S_z$ basis

---
### Solution
Since $J_z = \frac{\hbar}{2}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix}$ in the $S_z$ basis:

$$\hat{R}(\varphi) = e^{-iJ_z\varphi/\hbar} = e^{-i\varphi/2 \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix}} = e^{\mp i\varphi/2}$$in the $S_z$ basis.
$$\hat{R}(\varphi) \xrightarrow[S_z \text{ basis}]{} \begin{pmatrix} \langle +z|e^{-i\varphi/2}|+z\rangle & \langle +z|e^{i\varphi/2}|-z\rangle \\ \langle -z|e^{-i\varphi/2}|+z\rangle & \langle -z|e^{i\varphi/2}|-z\rangle \end{pmatrix} =\begin{pmatrix} e^{-i\varphi/2} \langle +z|+z\rangle & e^{i\varphi/2}\langle +z|-z\rangle \\ e^{-i\varphi/2}\langle -z|+z\rangle & e^{i\varphi/2}\langle -z|-z\rangle \end{pmatrix} = $$
$$=\begin{pmatrix} e^{-i\varphi/2} & 0 \\ 0 & e^{i\varphi/2}\end{pmatrix}$$
 
This is because $|+z\rangle$ is an eigenstate of $J_z$ with eigenvalue $+\hbar/2$: $$J_z|+z\rangle = \frac{\hbar}{2}|+z\rangle$$ Therefore: $$e^{-iJ_z\varphi/\hbar}|+z\rangle = e^{-i\varphi/2}|+z\rangle$$ So: $$R_{11} = e^{-i\varphi/2}\langle +z|+z\rangle = e^{-i\varphi/2}$$ Similarly: $$R_{22} = \langle -z|e^{-iJ_z\varphi/\hbar}|-z\rangle = e^{i\varphi/2}$$ The off-diagonal terms are zero because $|+z\rangle$ and $|-z\rangle$ are orthogonal.
$$\boxed{\hat{R}(\varphi) = \begin{bmatrix} e^{-i\varphi/2} & 0 \\ 0 & e^{i\varphi/2} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} \cos\frac{\varphi}{2} - i\sin\frac{\varphi}{2} & 0 \\ 0 & \cos\frac{\varphi}{2} + i\sin\frac{\varphi}{2} \end{pmatrix}}$$
