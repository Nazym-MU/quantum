The state

$$
|\psi\rangle = \frac{1}{2}|+z\rangle + \frac{i\sqrt{3}}{2}|-z\rangle
$$

is a state with $S_n = \hbar/2$ along a particular axis $\mathbf{n}$. Compare the state $|\psi\rangle$ with the state $|+n\rangle$ in Problem 1.3 to find $\mathbf{n}$.  Determine $\langle S_x \rangle$, $\langle S_y \rangle$, and $\langle S_z \rangle$ for this state.

---
### Solution
$$
\langle S_z \rangle = 
\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2\left(\frac{\hbar}{2}\right)
+ \left(\frac{i\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)\left(-\frac{\hbar}{2}\right)
= \frac{\hbar}{8} - \frac{3\hbar}{8}
= -\frac{\hbar}{4}
$$
---
$S_x$:
$$
\langle +x| = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\langle +z| + \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\langle -z|
$$
$$
\langle +x|\psi\rangle = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{2}} + \frac{i\sqrt{3}}{2\sqrt{2}}
= \frac{1 + i\sqrt{3}}{2\sqrt{2}}
$$
$$
|\langle +x|\psi\rangle|^2
= \frac{1 + i\sqrt{3}}{2\sqrt{2}} \cdot \frac{1 - i\sqrt{3}}{2\sqrt{2}}
= \frac{1 + 3}{8}
= \frac{1}{2}
$$
$$
\langle S_x \rangle =
\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{\hbar}{2}
- \frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{\hbar}{2}
= 0
$$
---
$S_y$:
$$
\langle +y| = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\langle +z| + \frac{i}{\sqrt{2}}\langle -z|
$$
$$
\langle +y|\psi\rangle =
\frac{1}{2\sqrt{2}} + \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2\sqrt{2}}
= \frac{1 + \sqrt{3}}{2\sqrt{2}}
$$
$$
|\langle +y|\psi\rangle|^2 =
\frac{4 + 2\sqrt{3}}{8}
= \frac{1}{2} + \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}
$$

$$
|\langle -y|\psi\rangle|^2 =
\frac{1}{2} - \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}
$$
$$
\langle S_y \rangle =
\left(\frac{1}{2} + \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}\right)\frac{\hbar}{2}
+ \left(\frac{1}{2} - \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}\right)\left(-\frac{\hbar}{2}\right)
= \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}\hbar
$$
