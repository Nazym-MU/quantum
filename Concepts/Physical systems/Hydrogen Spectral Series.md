Classification of spectral lines in hydrogen based on final state.
## General Formula
Photon emitted when electron transitions from $n_i$ to $n_f$:
$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H Z^2 \left(\frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2}\right)$$
where $R_H = \frac{\mu c \alpha^2}{2\hbar}$ is the Rydberg constant.
## Lyman Series ($n_f = 1$)
**Transitions**: $n_i = 2, 3, 4, \ldots \to n_f = 1$ (to ground state)
**Spectral region**: Ultraviolet (UV)
**Energy range**: Most energetic photons (10.2 - 13.6 eV)
**Example**: Lyman-$\alpha$ ($n_i = 2 \to n_f = 1$): $\lambda = 121.6$ nm
## Balmer Series ($n_f = 2$)
**Transitions**: $n_i = 3, 4, 5, \ldots \to n_f = 2$
**Spectral region**: Visible light
**Energy range**: 1.9 - 3.4 eV
**Examples**:
- H$_\alpha$ ($3 \to 2$): $\lambda = 656.3$ nm (red)
- H$_\beta$ ($4 \to 2$): $\lambda = 486.1$ nm (blue-green)
- H$_\gamma$ ($5 \to 2$): $\lambda = 434.0$ nm (violet)
This series was first observed and analyzed, leading to the Rydberg formula.
## Paschen Series ($n_f = 3$)
**Transitions**: $n_i = 4, 5, 6, \ldots \to n_f = 3$
**Spectral region**: Infrared (IR)
**Energy range**: Least energetic of these series (0.66 - 1.51 eV)
**Example**: Paschen-$\alpha$ ($4 \to 3$): $\lambda = 1875$ nm
## Series Limit
Each series has a **series limit** as $n_i \to \infty$:
$$\frac{1}{\lambda_{\text{limit}}} = \frac{R_H}{n_f^2}$$
Beyond this, the spectrum becomes continuous (ionization continuum).
