For infinitesimal rotation by dφ about z-axis:
$$R̂(d\phi \hat k) = 1 - \frac{i}{\hbar}\hat L_z d\phi$$
where $\hat L_z$ is the generator of rotations about the z-axis.
## Derivation from Position Space Rotations
An infinitesimal rotation about z-axis transforms coordinates:
- x → x - y dφ
- y → y + x dφ  
- z → z
In operator form:
$$|x - y d\phi, y + x d\phi, z⟩ = [1 - \frac{i}{\hbar}\hat P_x(-y d\phi)][1 -  \frac{i}{\hbar}\hat P_y(x d\phi)]|x, y, z⟩$$
$$= [1 -  \frac{i}{\hbar}(x\hat P_y - y\hat P_x)d\phi]|x, y, z⟩$$
## Orbital Angular Momentum Operator
Comparing with $\hat R(d\phi \hat k) = 1 -  \frac{i}{\hbar}\hat L_z d\phi$:
$$\hat L_z = x\hat P_y - y\hat P_x$$
This is the z-component of $\hat L = \hat r \times \hat P$, the orbital angular momentum operator.
This angular momentum arises from spatial motion (position × momentum). It generates rotations that move position states around in space, distinct from intrinsic spin angular momentum.
