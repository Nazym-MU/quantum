$$J_+​=J_x​+iJ_y​,\qquad J_−​=J_x​−iJ_y​$$
- They shift the $m$ quantum number up or down when acting on eigenstates of $J_z$
$$J_\pm​∣j,m⟩=\hbar \sqrt{j(j+1)−m(m\pm1)}​∣j,m±1⟩$$
Based on commutation: $[J_z​,J_±​]=±\hbar J_±​$
- Raising/lowering preserves total angular momentum ($\lambda$) but shifts the projection along z ($m$).
- $\lambda = j(j+1)$ is the total angular momentum, $m$ is its z-projection
- j can be integer or half-integer
- There are a total of 2j + 1 states

#### Magnitude of angular momentum
$$|\hat{J}|=\sqrt{j(j+1)}​\hbar$$ comes from the eigenvalue of $J^2$: $\hat{J}^2\ket{j,m} = j(j+1)\hbar^2\ket{j,m}$
#### Raising and lowering operator coefficients [[(9)]]
$$c_\pm=\hbar\sqrt{j(j+1)-m(m\pm1)}$$
#### Matrix elements
- Choose a basis $\ket{j, m}$ for a fixed $j$
$$\braket{j,m' | \hat{J}_\pm | j, m} = c_\pm\delta_{m', m\pm1}$$
