The position-space representations ⟨x|n⟩ of [[Harmonic Oscillator]] energy eigenstates.

## Ground State

$$\langle x|0\rangle = \left(\frac{m\omega}{\pi\hbar}\right)^{1/4} \exp\left(-\frac{m\omega x^2}{2\hbar}\right)$$

This is a [[Gaussian Wave Packet]] centered at x = 0.
Derivation: [[Ground State Wave Function Derivation]]

## Excited States
Generated by applying the raising operator in position space:
$$\langle x|n\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{n!}}\left[\sqrt{\frac{m\omega}{2\hbar}}\left(x - \frac{\hbar}{m\omega}\frac{d}{dx}\right)\right]^n \langle x|0\rangle$$

Derivation: [[Harmonic Oscillator Excited State Wave Functions]]

**Examples**:
$$\langle x|1\rangle = \left(\frac{4m^3\omega^3}{\pi\hbar^3}\right)^{1/4} x \exp\left(-\frac{m\omega x^2}{2\hbar}\right)$$

$$\langle x|2\rangle = \left(\frac{m\omega}{\pi\hbar}\right)^{1/4} \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left(\frac{2m\omega x^2}{\hbar} - 1\right) \exp\left(-\frac{m\omega x^2}{2\hbar}\right)$$

## General Structure

The wave functions can be written as:
$$\langle x|n\rangle = N_n H_n\left(\sqrt{\frac{m\omega}{\hbar}}x\right) \exp\left(-\frac{m\omega x^2}{2\hbar}\right)$$

where Hₙ are Hermite polynomials and Nₙ are normalization constants.

## Properties

**Node structure**: |n⟩ has exactly n nodes (zeros) where ⟨x|n⟩ = 0. See: [[Node Structure in Energy Eigenstates]]

**Parity**: ⟨x|n⟩ has definite parity:
- Even n: ⟨-x|n⟩ = ⟨x|n⟩ (even function)
- Odd n: ⟨-x|n⟩ = -⟨x|n⟩ (odd function)

**Classical turning points**: The wave function extends beyond the [[Classical Turning Points]] xₙ = ±√((2n+1)ℏ/mω), exhibiting quantum tunneling into classically forbidden regions.

**Oscillatory behavior**: As n increases, ⟨x|n⟩ becomes more oscillatory due to larger kinetic energy. The curvature d²⟨x|n⟩/dx² increases with n.

Derivation: [[Kinetic Energy and Wave Function Curvature]]

## Probability Densities

$$|\langle x|n\rangle|^2 = \text{probability density to find particle at position } x$$

For large n, the quantum probability density approaches the classical probability distribution:
$$P_{\text{classical}}(x) \propto \frac{1}{|v(x)|} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{E_n - V(x)}}$$

## Normalization

All energy eigenstates are normalized:
$$\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} |\langle x|n\rangle|^2 dx = 1$$
