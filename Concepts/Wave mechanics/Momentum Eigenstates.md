Momentum eigenstates |p⟩ satisfy:
$$\hat{p}_x|p\rangle = p|p\rangle$$
where p ∈ ℝ ranges over all possible momenta.
## Key Properties
**Completeness:**
$$\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} dp \, |p\rangle\langle p| = \hat{I}$$
**Orthonormality:**
$$\langle p|p'\rangle = \delta(p-p')$$
## Position-Space Representation
The wave function of a momentum eigenstate in position space:
$$\langle x|p\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\hbar}}e^{ipx/\hbar}$$
[[Derivation of <x p>]]
## Physical Interpretation
**In position space:**
- Complex plane wave extending over all space
- |⟨x|p⟩|² = 1/(2πℏ) = constant (completely delocalized)
- Δx = ∞, Δp = 0
**In momentum space:**
- ⟨p'|p⟩ = δ(p'-p): infinitely sharp spike
- Perfectly definite momentum
**Not physical:** Cannot normalize to unit probability in position space.
## de Broglie Wavelength
From e^(ipx/ℏ) = cos(px/ℏ) + i·sin(px/ℏ):
Phase increases by 2π when x increases by λ:
$$\frac{p\lambda}{\hbar} = 2\pi \quad \Rightarrow \quad \lambda = \frac{h}{p}$$
