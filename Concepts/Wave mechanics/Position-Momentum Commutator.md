$$[\hat{x}, \hat{p}_x] = i\hbar$$
## Derivation from Translation
Starting from $T̂(δx) = 1 - (i/ℏ)p̂_xδx$ and $[x̂, \hat T(δx)] =\delta x$:
$$[\hat{x}, \hat{p}_x] = i\hbar$$
## Physical Consequences
**Incompatibility:**
$x̂$ and $p̂_x$ cannot be simultaneously measured with arbitrary precision.
**Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle:**
$$\Delta x \cdot \Delta p_x \geq \frac{\hbar}{2}$$
This follows directly from the general uncertainty relation:
$$\Delta A \cdot \Delta B \geq \frac{1}{2}|\langle[\hat{A},\hat{B}]\rangle|$$

##Useful Related Commutators
$$[\hat{p}_x, \hat{x}] = -i\hbar$$
$$[\hat{p}_x^2, \hat{x}] = -2i\hbar\hat{p}_x$$
$$[\hat{x}, V(\hat{x})] = 0$$
$$[\hat{p}_x, V(\hat{x})] = -i\hbar\frac{dV}{dx}$$
