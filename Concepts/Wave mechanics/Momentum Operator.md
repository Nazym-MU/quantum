The momentum operator $p̂$ₓ is the generator of spatial translations.
## In Position Basis
$$\hat{p}_x = \frac{\hbar}{i}\frac{\partial}{\partial x} = -i\hbar\frac{\partial}{\partial x}$$
**Acting on wave functions:**
$$\langle x|\hat{p}_x|\psi\rangle = -i\hbar\frac{\partial\psi(x)}{\partial x}$$
## Momentum Eigenstates
$$\hat{p}_x|p\rangle = p|p\rangle$$
**Position representation:**
$$\langle x|p\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\hbar}}e^{ipx/\hbar}$$
This is a plane wave with wavelength λ = h/p (de Broglie relation).
## Physical Interpretation
- Hermitian operator → observable (momentum measurements)
- Generator of translations → tells how position changes
- Classical limit: ⟨$p̂$ₓ⟩/m = d⟨x⟩/dt (Ehrenfest theorem)
## Connection to Classical Physics
From Heisenberg equation with $Ĥ = p̂^2/(2m) + V(x̂)$:
$$\frac{d\langle x\rangle}{dt} = \frac{\langle p_x\rangle}{m} \qquad \frac{d\langle p_x\rangle}{dt} = -\left\langle\frac{dV}{dx}\right\rangle$$
